If you or someone you care about is facing a mental health crisis in the Philippines, there is free, immediate support available through five different hotlines and helplines. These services provide confidential counseling by phone, text, or online chat, and are often referred to as crisis lines. They are designed to offer a safe, nonjudgmental space where you can talk openly, process emotions, and find meaningful next steps. The people who answer these calls are usually trained volunteers, counselors, or peers with lived experience, and they use skills like active listening and crisis counseling to provide support. Helplines can assist with a wide range of issues, including emotional distress, anxiety, depression, abuse, domestic violence, and suicidal thoughts, while some focus on specific groups such as children, teens, veterans, or the LGBTQ+ community. Suicide hotlines in particular are available 24/7 to those who are struggling with thoughts of self-harm or ending their life. You can also call these hotlines if you‚Äôre worried about a loved one, and they will guide you on how to offer support or connect them to outside resources. Helplines in the Philippines, like thousands of others worldwide, share a common goal: to help people in distress find hope, stability, and the right support when it‚Äôs needed most.

NCMH crisis hotline

NCMH Crisis Hotline The NCMH Crisis Hotline is available 24/7 to provide free and confidential support for people experiencing emotional distress, mental health concerns, or crises. You can reach them by calling

1800-1888-1553.

Tawag Paglaum - Centro Bisaya

HOPELINE offers around-the-clock assistance for individuals dealing with anxiety, depression, suicidal thoughts, and other mental health challenges. Their services are free, confidential, and accessible through-

(02) 8804-4673.

In Touch: Crisis Line

This hotline is dedicated to helping people who are struggling with emotional and suicidal crises. It is available 24/7 to anyone in need of immediate support. You can contact them at-

0966-467-9626.

HOPELINE

The In Touch Crisis Line provides 24/7 emotional support through trained volunteers who are skilled in active listening and crisis counseling. They can be reached at-

+63 2 8893 7603.

BANTAY BATA helpline 163

Bantay Bata Helpline 163 focuses on supporting children, youth, and parents who may be facing emotional difficulties, abuse, or other crises. Their service is available daily from 7 AM to 7 PM and can be accessed by dialing 163 or by emailing-

bantaybata163@abs-cbnfoundation.com
