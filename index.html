What exactly is stigma?

Stigma refers to the negative beliefs, attitudes, and stereotypes that people have against people with mental health conditions. Discrimination toward people living with mental incapabilities, osctrasizing them.

The word stigma itself means a mark, stain, or blemish. Applied to mental health, it describes the way people are unfairly labeled or stereotyped based on their symptoms or diagnosis.

Because of stigma, many people hesitate to seek treetment out of fear of being judged, losing opportunities, or being treated differently. Whether it shows up openly or in subtle ways, stigma harms people by reducing access to care and isolating them from support.

Those living with mental illness often face stigma. They may be seen in a negative light, treated differently, or made to feel embarrassed or ashamed about their condition. Stigma can also result in discrimination at work, in school, or in social situations which is further worsening their struggles.
